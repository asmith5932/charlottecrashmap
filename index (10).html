Charlotte's Vision Zero analysis found that 13% of streets account for 80% of fatal and serious crashes, and that people walking and biking make up 29% of severe injuries. This map takes an independent look at that concentration using network kernel density estimation.

About this map

Charlotte severe-crash density

A network-based view of where killed-or-seriously-injured (KSI) crashes concentrate across Charlotte's streets, with detail on fourteen of the highest-injury corridors.

What the colors show

The warm ramp is a network kernel density estimate (NKDE) of KSI crashes — a measure of how tightly severe crashes cluster along the street network. Gray streets carry little or no severe-crash density; yellow, orange, and red mark rising concentration. Density classes match the published analysis.

Corridor statistics

For each highlighted corridor, the panel shows fatalities, serious injuries, total KSI, and KSI per mile (counts normalized by corridor length). Per-mile rates let short and long corridors be compared on equal footing; because some corridors are short, their per-mile rates are more sensitive to individual crashes and should be read as indicative rather than precise. Corridors that share an intersection may double-count crashes occurring at that junction.

Data source

The 13% / 80% / 29% figures are the City of Charlotte's own findings, from its FY25 Vision Zero Annual Report, and describe the city's High-Injury Network — a separate analysis from the network kernel density estimate shown on this map.
